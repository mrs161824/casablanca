Our Story

A riad courtyard,
reimagined for Cardiff evenings.

Casablanca started with a simple idea: bring the unhurried hospitality of a Moroccan riad to South Wales. Copper trays, hand-thrown tagine pots and a kitchen that starts its stock at dawn — everything here is built for slow, generous meals shared with people you like.

Our chefs cook the dishes they grew up with: lamb that falls from the bone after hours over charcoal, couscous steamed the traditional way, and pastries brushed with orange-blossom honey. Every table gets mint tea poured from height, the way it's done in Marrakech's medina.
